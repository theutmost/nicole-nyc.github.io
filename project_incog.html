Redesign of Blue Link’s iOS & Android app

iOS App UX/Architecture for the users privacy

Overview

My Senior Project/Thesis in Otis College of Art and Design was about Mobile Device and Personal Data. After a semester of researching and writing a paper about the topic, my solution to this matter was to create Incog, a better guided iOS application for the users' privacy. Incog speaks to the iPhone users how much of personal information can be stored online through using the downloaded applications and web browsing behaviors.

Also, Incog makes suggestions of the method to keep their personal data protected and safe by paying attention to 3rd party app privacy settings, deleting all cookies and permissions on all iPhone platforms (safari, iCloud, etc) and using Virtual Private Network Service.

Blue Link’s significant page - Vehicle Status

As a designer, my duties were to dissect the client requirements, to create low to high fidelity wireframes, to present designs to the client, and to support developers after client’s design approval. I worked closely with the Developers and Project Managers to ensure requirements and UI asset production timetables were met.

• All Designs were executed in a fast-paced, dynamic environment
• Closely Collaborated with the client team to better understand users expectations and needs in specific market (Korea) and made design solutions with technological possibilities and limitations in mind.

Requirement Analysis

As a communicator and a collaborator, I connected productively between the client and the developers by translating requirements into the interface while documenting the complex back-end logic and information for methodical development.

• Collaborated with clients to enhance the product with the effective visuals and copies with innovative concepts which were strongly connected with the client technology, in-vehicle headunit system.
• Worked with engineers to implement and iterate on designs.
• Report developers' issues or questions to the client team as necessary.
• Provided specifications, made small to big user experience flow/design decisions and its guidance as necessary.

New Features

User testing is a technique used in user-centered interaction design to evaluate a product by testing it on users. This can be seen as an irreplaceable usability practice, since it gives direct input on how real users use the system. So we decided to use this technique in our app with this tasks:

  1. Bio Recognition Login
  2. HVAC - Heat/Vent Seat Settings
  3. Set Schedule - for the vehicle type
  4. Charge Limit Settings
  5. EV Mobile Charging Service
  6. ECO Drive Usage
  7. Hydrogen Station Detail Page
  8. Creating Route
  9. Calendar Sync Notifications
  10. AR Navi to Parking Location
  11. Navi (Car Address Book) Sync
  12. Vehicle Update Notifications
  13. Personal Settings Back-up

Wireframing & Prototyping

With the proper planning and analysing the requirement analysing the connected logic, I was able to confidently move into creating wireframes for some of the new features. I took the decision to focus more on the functionality and structure of the app, opting for low-fidelity wireframes with little UI detail.

Final Step: Visual Design

One major change in the app, was to focus on the flat style UI. Our aim was to improve user delight by giving the modern look with its distinguishing brand color.

Blue Link’s final User Interface: some features are different by its connected vehicle type

Results

The project started in the beginning of 2017 and launched in Korea for both iOS and Android in April 2018. Blue Link is a aesthetic mobile application that was able to perform the business tasks.
